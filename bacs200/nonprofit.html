The Trevor Project

Saving Young LGBTQ Lives

History and Film:

Peggy Rajski and Randy Stone saw performer James Lecesne play the character of Trevor, an origianl character he created for the show WORD OF MOUTH. Rajski and Stone believed the stor would make a great short-film and invited Lecesne to then go on to create an Oscar-winning film. After appearing on the Ellen DeGeneres show, Rajski realized there was no place for young people like Trevor to get help and support. The night their film was to premiere on HBO, the filmmakers launched the Trevor Lifeline, the first notional crisis intervention and suicide prevention lifeline for LGBTQ youth.

Programs and Services:

  • Trevor Lifeline
  • LGBTQ on Campus
  • Trevor Ally Training
  • Trevor Advocacy Network
  • Coming Out: A Handbook for LGBTQ Young People

Strategies Plan and Mission

  • The Mission of the Trevor Project is to end suicide among gay, lesbian, bisexual, transgender, queer, and questioning young people. LGBTQ young people are 4 times more likely to attempt suicide compared to their peers and the second leading cause of death among young people in the entirety of the United States. The Trevor Proejct is not the worlds leading organization responding to the crisis of LGBTQ youth suicide with a 24/7 lifeline and thousands ready to help.
